Tasty Margherita Pizza

Margherita Pizza is a classic Italian pizza that has become a beloved favorite around the world. The pizza is named after Queen Margherita of Savoy, who reportedly fell in love with the pizza during a visit to Naples in 1889. The pizza features a thin, crispy crust topped with a simple tomato sauce, fresh mozzarella cheese, and fresh basil leaves. Margherita Pizza is a perfect example of the beauty of Italian cuisine in its simplicity and high-quality ingredients.

Ingredients:

  • 1 pound pizza dough (homemade or store-bought)
  • 1/2 cup tomato sauce
  • 8 ounces fresh mozzarella cheese, sliced
  • 1/4 cup fresh basil leaves
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Olive oil for brushing

Cooking Instructions

  1. Preheat the oven to 450°F (230°C). If you have a pizza stone, place it in the oven to preheat as well.
  2. On a floured surface, roll out the pizza dough to the desired thickness and shape.
  3. Spread the tomato sauce over the pizza dough, leaving a small border around the edges for the crust.
  4. Arrange the sliced mozzarella cheese over the tomato sauce.
  5. Sprinkle salt and pepper over the cheese to taste.
  6. Drizzle a little bit of olive oil over the pizza.
  7. Transfer the pizza to the preheated pizza stone or a baking sheet.
  8. Bake the pizza in the preheated oven for 10-12 minutes, or until the crust is crispy and the cheese is melted and bubbly.
  9. Remove the pizza from the oven and top with fresh basil leaves.
  10. Let the pizza cool for a few minutes before slicing and serving.

Note: For a crispier crust, you can brush the edges of the pizza dough with olive oil before adding the toppings. You can also sprinkle a little bit of cornmeal on the pizza stone or baking sheet to prevent the dough from sticking.
