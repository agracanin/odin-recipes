Spaghetti alla Carbonara

Spaghetti alla Carbonara is a classic Italian pasta dish that is beloved around the world. The dish features spaghetti noodles coated in a rich and creamy sauce made from eggs, bacon or pancetta, Parmesan cheese, and black pepper. Spaghetti alla Carbonara is a simple yet satisfying dish that can be whipped up in just a few minutes, making it perfect for a quick weeknight dinner.

Ingredients:

  • 1 pound spaghetti
  • 6 ounces bacon or pancetta, diced
  • 4 cloves garlic, minced
  • 4 eggs
  • 1 cup grated Parmesan cheese, plus extra for serving
  • 1 teaspoon freshly ground black pepper
  • Salt to taste
  • Fresh parsley for garnish (optional)

Cooking Instructions:

  1. Bring a large pot of salted water to a boil. Add the spaghetti noodles and cook according to package instructions until al dente.
  2. While the pasta cooks, cook the bacon or pancetta in a large skillet over medium-high heat until crispy. Add the minced garlic to the skillet and cook for an additional minute until fragrant.
  3. In a medium bowl, whisk together the eggs, Parmesan cheese, black pepper, and a pinch of salt.
  4. When the pasta is done cooking, reserve 1 cup of the pasta cooking water, then drain the spaghetti.
  5. Add the hot spaghetti to the skillet with the bacon or pancetta and toss to combine.
  6. Remove the skillet from heat and pour the egg mixture over the spaghetti, tossing quickly to coat the noodles. If the pasta seems dry, add a little bit of the reserved pasta cooking water to the skillet and toss again.
  7. Serve the spaghetti alla Carbonara hot, topped with extra Parmesan cheese and fresh parsley if desired.

Note: Be sure to toss the pasta quickly with the egg mixture to prevent the eggs from scrambling. If you're concerned about the raw eggs, you can cook the egg mixture in a double boiler until thickened before adding it to the pasta.
