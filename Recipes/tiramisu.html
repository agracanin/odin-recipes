Tiramisu

Tiramisu is a classic Italian dessert that is known for its rich and creamy layers of mascarpone cheese, espresso-soaked ladyfingers, and dusting of cocoa powder. The dessert has a smooth and silky texture and a subtle coffee flavor that is perfectly balanced with the sweetness of the mascarpone cheese. Tiramisu is a perfect dessert for any occasion, and is sure to impress your guests.

Ingredients:

  • 6 egg yolks
  • 3/4 cup granulated sugar
  • 2/3 cup mascarpone cheese, at room temperature
  • 1 1/4 cups heavy cream
  • 1 cup strong brewed espresso, cooled
  • 2 tablespoons rum or marsala wine (optional)
  • 24-30 ladyfingers
  • Cocoa powder for dusting

Cooking Instructions:

  1. In a medium bowl, whisk together the egg yolks and sugar until light and fluffy.
  2. Add the mascarpone cheese to the bowl and beat until smooth.
  3. In a separate large bowl, beat the heavy cream until stiff peaks form.
  4. Fold the whipped cream into the mascarpone mixture until well combined.
  5. In a shallow dish, combine the cooled espresso and rum or marsala wine (if using).
  6. Dip each ladyfinger into the espresso mixture for a few seconds, making sure to coat each side evenly.
  7. Arrange a layer of the soaked ladyfingers in the bottom of a 9-inch square baking dish.
  8. Spread half of the mascarpone mixture over the layer of ladyfingers.
  9. Repeat with another layer of soaked ladyfingers and the remaining mascarpone mixture.
  10. Cover the baking dish with plastic wrap and refrigerate for at least 2 hours, or overnight.
  11. Before serving, dust the top of the Tiramisu with cocoa powder.

Note: Tiramisu can be made ahead of time and stored in the refrigerator for up to 2 days. You can also use a mixture of coffee and cocoa powder instead of espresso if you prefer.
